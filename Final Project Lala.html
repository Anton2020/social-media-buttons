Asian Curly Hair:
Redefining Beauty Standards

Author: Lala Fitriani
January 24, 2020

Sandra Oh from Grey's Anatomy

For many years, many people think that Asians can't have curly hair. There's even a definition of how "Asian hair" should look like: black, straight, sleek and silky. But that's not true! There are so many Asians who have textured hair, from slightly wavy to very curly (aka kinky) and it's time to for us to break the myth!

MY STORY

My name is Lala, I'm from Indonesia and I have naturally curly hair. Some people might not be familiar with my country, or at most they might be more familiar with Bali. Well, if you know Bali, it is one of the thousands islands that are located in Indonesia.
Unlike the famous Bali, I live in a small village far away from the bif cities called Pulosari, Central Java. In Indonesia, many people still think that curly hair is ugly and living in a small village makes it worse. My family and friends embrace the beauty standard set by the society: skinny body, white skin and silky straight hair. But, I wasn't born like that.
I have dark skin, curly hair and a body that is considered unideal in my society. Throughout my childhood until my high school years, I was bullied simply because I was born "different."
So one day, I decided to chemically straighten my hair, it made me feel really really good and almost as if a chain was being released off my neck. Finally, I "fit" in the beauty standard. And that moment, I was happy. But then, I began to worry again and again every time my roots started to grow. Every six months or so, I would go to salon to touch up my hair and got it straightened again. I only cared about what people think. And, I wasn't happy anymore, I was tired.
Until June last year, I decided to learn how to straighten my hair at home and watched a video on youtube, and this was literally how my curly hair journey began. I read a comment where someone shared an Indonesian youtuber that shared lots of informations on how to take care of naturally curly hair, and I decided to binge watch her videos, and, the rest is history. (I'm forever thankful to that youtube comment)
This June, I'm one year curly. I can't believe I saved myself form the beauty standard that once held me captive for years. Now, I love myself more than ever before. Because, this curly hair journey doesn't only teach me to take care of my curly hair, but also to accept my self for who I am, and to always try to be better, not for anyone else, but for myself.
Right now, I'm also playing my part to help promote naturally curly hair to Asian people, especially Indonesians who still think that curly hair is nothing but ugly. The Asian myth that says that all Asians have silky straight hair also has to be destroyed, because that is simply not true. We all have different hair types (including Asian people) which includes straight, wavy, curly or kinky. We all should try to embrace them.
